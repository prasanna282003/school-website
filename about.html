About Our School

Our Legacy of Excellence

Khidwai English School is a vibrant learning environment that holds a deep reverence for and understanding of the development of the child as a physical, intellectual, social and spiritual being.

At Khidwai English School, we believe that from their earliest years of schooling through early adolescence, children are forming the skills and attitudes that will drive all future academic achievement and social development.

We blend school traditions with academic excellence in an environment that inspires the imagination, fosters creativity, and promotes a love of learning that lasts a lifetime.

Mission and Objectives

Our Motto: Love Conquers Everything

We live and breathe our motto to educate children in mind, body, and spirit every day, just as we have since our founding in 1990. Our commitment enables us to help a child of promise grow into a being of character.

Our mission is to care for our students, and to educate them in accordance with clearly stated values; to bring out the best in every one of them; and to help them to take the next step in their lives with confidence, conviction and consideration for others.

Objectives

  • Educate and nurture children from pre-kg to tenth grade using the Khidwai philosophy and curriculum.
  • Create a diverse and supportive environment; based on the principles of healthy social development.
  • Provide educational opportunities, focusing on both curricular and co-curricular activities.
  • To be a best Co-ed school positioned to offer children varieties of program designed to address their specific needs.
  • Foster a culture of respect, responsibility, and lifelong learning among students.
  • Develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills through innovative teaching methods.

Principal's Message

A Word From Our Principal

"At Khidwai English School, we believe that education is not just about academic excellence, but about shaping character, building confidence, and nurturing the unique potential within each child."

Learning happens when we learn directly about a new culture…not from a textbook but from our roommate, or a new friend. Learning grows out of our successes and our failures. It happens when we study, work, talk, play, pray, share, reflect, argue…and when we make up.

Granted, it doesn't happen all the time, but a community that is imbued with the spirit of learning finds that learning is an ongoing continuous process that extends far beyond the classroom walls.

— The Principal
